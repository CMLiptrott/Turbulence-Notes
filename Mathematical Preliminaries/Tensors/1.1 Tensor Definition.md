Let $\boldsymbol{T}$ be a transformation, which transforms any vector into another vector. If $\boldsymbol{T}$ is linear and homogenous (of degree 1), i.e. if  $$\boldsymbol{T(\alpha \boldsymbol{a}+ \beta \boldsymbol{b})} = \alpha \boldsymbol{T} \boldsymbol{a} + \beta\boldsymbol{T }\boldsymbol{b}, $$ we call $\boldsymbol{T}$ a tensor. 


Other names: Linear Transformation, Second-order Tensor.
