Let the vector $\boldsymbol{a} = a_i  \boldsymbol{e}_i$. Then $\boldsymbol{b} = \boldsymbol{T} \boldsymbol{a}$ is a vector given by $$
 \mathbf{b}=\mathbf{T}\mathbf{a}=a_{1}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{1}+a_{2}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{2}+a_{3}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{3}.
$$Here, $\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{i}$ is the vector resulting from the action of $\mathbf{T}$ on $\mathbf{e}_{i}.$ The components of $\mathbf{b}$ are given by $$
\begin{align*}
b_{1}&=\mathbf{b}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{1}=a_{1}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{1}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{1} + a_{2}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{2}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{1}+a_{3}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{3}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{1} \\

b_{2}&=\mathbf{b}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{2}=a_{1}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{1}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{2} + a_{2}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{2}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{2}+a_{3}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{3}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{2} \\

b_{2}&=\mathbf{b}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{3}=a_{1}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{1}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{3} + a_{2}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{2}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{3}+a_{3}\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{3}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{3}

\end{align*}
$$
Note that $\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{j} \cdot \mathbf{e}_{i} = \mathbf{e}_{i}\cdot \mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{j}$ is unambiguous.  More compactly, $$
b_{i} = a_{j}\mathbf{e}_{i}\cdot \mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{j}.
$$
We define $$
T_{ij} = \mathbf{e}_{i}\cdot \mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{j}.
$$
So we can write $$
b_{i} = T_{ij}a_{j}.
$$
We can write this in the following matrix form $$
\begin{bmatrix}
b_{1} \\
b_{2} \\
b_{3} 
\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}
T_{11} & T_{12} & T_{13} \\
T_{21} & T_{22} & T_{23} \\ 
T_{31} & T_{32} & T_{33} \\
\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}
a_{1} \\
a_{2} \\
a_{3}
\end{bmatrix}
$$
The matrix $T_{ij}$ is the **matrix of the tensor $\mathbf{T}$** with respect to the set of unit vectors $\{\mathbf{e}_{1},\mathbf{e}_{2},\mathbf{e}_{3}\}$. Notice that the components of $\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{i}$ are given by the $i$-th column, i.e. $\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{i}=T_{ji}\mathbf{e}_{j}.$

A tensor does not depend on any coordinate system but its components do (like vectors).
