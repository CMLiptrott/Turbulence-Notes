The **transpose of a tensor $\mathbf{T}$** denoted by $\mathbf{T}^T$ is defined to be that which satisfies the following identity 
$$
\mathbf{a}\cdot(\mathbf{T}\mathbf{b}) = \mathbf{b}\cdot(\mathbf{T}^T\mathbf{a}).
$$

We therefore have 
$$
\mathbf{e}_{i}\cdot(\mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{j}) = \mathbf{e}_{j}\cdot(\mathbf{T}^T\mathbf{e}_{i}),
$$

So 
$$
T_{ij} = T^T_{ji}.
$$

i.e. the matrix of $\mathbf{T}^T$ is the transpose of the matrix of $\mathbf{T}$. 

Claim: $(\mathbf{S}\mathbf{T})^T = \mathbf{T}^T\mathbf{S}^T$
Proof: If we can establish 
$$
\mathbf{a}\cdot(\mathbf{S}\mathbf{T})\mathbf{b} = \mathbf{b}\cdot(\mathbf{T}^T\mathbf{S}^T)\mathbf{a}
$$
Then the claim is established, by definition of the transpose. 
First, we have 
$$
\mathbf{a}\cdot(\mathbf{S}\mathbf{T})\mathbf{b}=\mathbf{a}\cdot \mathbf{S}(\mathbf{T}\mathbf{b}).
$$
Let $\mathbf{c}=\mathbf{T}\mathbf{b}$, so 
$$
\mathbf{a}\cdot \mathbf{S}\mathbf{c} = \mathbf{c}\cdot \mathbf{S}^\mathbf{T}\mathbf{a} = \mathbf{T}\mathbf{b}\cdot \mathbf{S}^T\mathbf{a}
$$

Now let $\mathbf{d}=\mathbf{S}^T\mathbf{a}$, so 
$$
\mathbf{T}\mathbf{b}\cdot \mathbf{d} = \mathbf{d}\cdot \mathbf{T}\mathbf{b} = \mathbf{b}\cdot \mathbf{T}^T\mathbf{d} = \mathbf{b}\cdot \mathbf{T}^T(\mathbf{S}^T\mathbf{a}) = \mathbf{b}\cdot (\mathbf{T}^T\mathbf{S}^T)\mathbf{a}
$$
as required.
