If $\mathbf{T}$ and $\mathbf{S}$ are tensors, then the product $\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S}$ and $\mathbf{S}\mathbf{T}$ are defined to be the transformations 
$$
\begin{align}
(\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S})\mathbf{a} &= \mathbf{T}(\mathbf{S}\mathbf{a}), \\
(\mathbf{S}\mathbf{T})\mathbf{a} &=\mathbf{S}(\mathbf{T}\mathbf{a}).  
\end{align}
$$
These are clearly tensors: 
$$
 (\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S})(\alpha \mathbf{a}+\beta \mathbf{b}) = \mathbf{T}(\mathbf{S}(\alpha \mathbf{a}+\beta \mathbf{b})) = \mathbf{T}(\alpha \mathbf{S}\mathbf{a} + \beta \mathbf{S}\mathbf{b}) = \alpha (\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S})\mathbf{a}+\beta (\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S})\mathbf{b}.
$$

The components of $\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S}$ are
$$\begin{align}
(\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S})_{ij} &= \mathbf{e}_{i}\cdot(\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S})\mathbf{e}_{j} = \mathbf{e}_{i}\cdot \mathbf{T}(\mathbf{S}\mathbf{e}_{j})  \\
&= \mathbf{e}_{i}\cdot \mathbf{T}(S_{mj}\mathbf{e}_{m}) = S_{mj}(\mathbf{e}_{i}\cdot \mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{m}) \\
 &= S_{mj}(\mathbf{e}_{i}\cdot \mathbf{T}\mathbf{e}_{m}) = T_{im}S_{mj}
\end{align}
$$
Notice that this is the same as matrix multiplication 
$$
[\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S}] = [\mathbf{T}][\mathbf{S}].
$$

So the rules of matric multiplication apply, e.g. in general $[\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S}]\neq[\mathbf{S}\mathbf{T}]$. 

Claim: Tensor products are associative
Proof: If $\mathbf{T}$, $\mathbf{S}$ and $\mathbf{V}$ are tensors, then 
$$
(\mathbf{T}(\mathbf{S}\mathbf{V}))\mathbf{a} = \mathbf{T}((\mathbf{S}\mathbf{V})\mathbf{a}) = \mathbf{T}(\mathbf{S}(\mathbf{V}\mathbf{a}))
$$
and 
$$
 (\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S})(\mathbf{V}\mathbf{a}) = \mathbf{T}(\mathbf{S}(\mathbf{V}\mathbf{a}))
$$

So 
$$
\mathbf{T}(\mathbf{S}\mathbf{V}) = (\mathbf{T}\mathbf{S})\mathbf{V}
$$

i.e. tensor products are associative.
