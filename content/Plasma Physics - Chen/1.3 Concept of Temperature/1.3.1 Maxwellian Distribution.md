A gas in thermal equilibrium has particles of all velocities. The most probably distribution of these velocities is known as the Maxwellian distribution. The one dimensional Maxwellian distribution is given by
<p style="text-transform: uppercase; font-size: 0.8em; font-weight: bold; margin-bottom: 0;text-align: center;">One-Dimensional Maxwellian Distribution</p>

$$
 f(u) = A\exp\left( -\frac{1}{2} \mu^2 /KT\right)
$$
$fdu$ is the number of particles per $m^3$ with velocity between $u$ and $u+du$. The kinetic energy is $\frac{1}{2}m u^2$. $K$ is Boltzmann's constant, 
<p style="text-transform: uppercase; font-size: 0.8em; font-weight: bold; margin-bottom: 0;text-align: center;">Boltzmann's Constant</p>

$$
K = 1.38 \times 10^{-23}J / ^\circ K
$$


Note: One-dimensional meaning a gas in which the particles can only move in one dimension.
