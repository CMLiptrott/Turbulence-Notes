
Since $E_{av}$ and $T$ are closely related, temperature is given in units of energy in plasma physics. The energy corresponding to $KT$ is used to denote the temperature, called the **thermal energy** of the plasma. Thermal energy is given in **electron volts**, where
$$
KT = 1eV = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}J
$$
is the conversion. Substituting Boltzmann's constant gives 
$$
T = \frac{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}{1.38 \times 10^{-23}} = 11600
$$
So the conversion factor is 
$$
1eV = 11600 ^\circ K.
$$
By a $2$-$eV$ plasma we mean $KT = 2eV$, or $E_{av} = 3 eV$ in three dimensions.
