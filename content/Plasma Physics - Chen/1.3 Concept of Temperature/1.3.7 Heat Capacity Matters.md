
Does a high temperature mean a lot of heat? No. The electron temperature inside a fluorescent light bulb is around $20000^\circ K$. The *heat capacity* must be taken into account. The density of electrons inside a fluorescent tube is much less than that of a gas at atmospheric pressure, and the total amount of heat transferred to the wall by electrons striking it at their thermal velocities is not that great.   

Many laboratory plasmas have temperatures of the order $1000000^\circ K$ but very low densities means the heating of the walls is not a serious consideration.
