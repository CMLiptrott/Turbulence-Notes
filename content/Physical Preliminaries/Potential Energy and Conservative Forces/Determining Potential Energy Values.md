Finding a general relationship between a conservative force and its associated potential energy. In general the work done on a varying path by a varying force is 
$$
W = \int_{P_{1}}^{P_{2}}\mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{l}
$$
So the change in potential is

$$
\Delta U = - \int_{P_{1}}^{P_{2}}\mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{l}
$$

This the general relation. 

**Gravitational Potential Energy**
$$
\Delta U = mg(y_{f}-y_{i})
$$
Only changes in $\Delta U$, or any other type of potential energy, are physically meaningful. However, sometimes we like to say that a certain gravitational potential value $U$ is associated with a certain particle-Earth system when the particle is at height $y$. So we write 
$$
U -U_{i} = mg(y-y_{i})
$$
then we take $U_{i}$ to be the gravitational potential energy of the system when it is in a **reference configuration** in which the particle is at a **reference point** $y_{i}$. Usually we take $U_{i}=0$ and $y_{i}=0$ and get 
$$
U(y) = mgy
$$
This tells us that the gravitational potential energy associated with a certain particle-Earth system depends only on the vertical location $y$ and not the horizontal location.
