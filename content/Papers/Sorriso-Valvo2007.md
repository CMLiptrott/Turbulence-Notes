# Observation of Inertial Energy Cascade in Interplanetary Space Plasma


Overall: ●●●●◐ 4.5/5
Readability: ●●●●● 5/5
Relevance: ●●●●◐ 4.5/5

PRL 99, 115001 (2007)

Evidence of inertial energy cascade in the solar wind from Ulysses Spacecraft. 


Start with Yagloms law for MHD - proportionality relation between the mixed third-order structure functions of the fields and the increment scale. Equivalent of Kolmogorov's law. The existence of such a relation is observed. 

Method
- write incompressible MHD in Elsasser variable form. then they have same structure as Navier-Stokes equations:
$$
\partial_{t}\mathbf{z}^\pm + \mathbf{z}^{\mp}\cdot \nabla \mathbf{z}^\pm = \nabla \frac{P}{\rho} + \left( \frac{\nu+\kappa}{2} \right)\nabla^{2}\mathbf{z}^\pm -  \left( \frac{\nu-\kappa}{2} \right)\nabla^{2}\mathbf{z}^\mp.
$$
	where $\nu$ is the viscosity, $\kappa$ is the magnetic diffusivity, and  $P$ is the [[Total Presure|total pressure]].  
- Assuming local homogeneity, a relation for the mixed third-order structure function can be obtained in the stationary state
$$
\partial_{\parallel}Y^{\pm}(r) = -\frac{4}{3}\epsilon^\pm + 2\nu \nabla^2\langle|\Delta z^\pm|^2\rangle - 2\langle \Delta \mathbf{z}^\pm \cdot(\nabla+\nabla')\Delta \frac{P}{\rho}\rangle + \langle \mathbf{z}^\mp \cdot (\nabla+\nabla')|\Delta z^\pm|^2 \rangle
$$
	where $\boldsymbol{\epsilon}^\pm = \nu\langle| \nabla \mathbf{z}^\pm|^2\rangle$ are the pseudoenergy average dissipation rates. 

- Last term disappears if globally homogeneous. Pressure term vanishes if locally isotropic. In the inertial range of MHD turbulence, $\nu\rightarrow 0$, a linear scaling law 
	$$
 Y^{\pm}(r) = -\frac{4}{3} \boldsymbol{\epsilon}^\pm r
$$
	 is obtained. This characterises a turbulent cascade with a well-defined finite energy flux $\mathbf{\epsilon}^\pm$.
	  
Claim:
Yaglom relation does indeed hold in some intervals of the solar wind.
